🎯 Deep Analysis: The "Closed" Circulation Trap

Most Molluscs have an open circulatory system, EXCEPT Cephalopods (Octopus, Squid) which have a closed system. Annelids generally have a closed system. Arthropods always have an open system. This distinction is a 5-star NEET target.

NEET 2023 Radial symmetry is NOT found in adults of which phylum?

Answer: Hemichordata. Adults of Cnidaria, Ctenophora, and Echinodermata exhibit radial symmetry. Hemichordates are strictly bilateral throughout their life cycle.

Animal Morphology — Frog & Cockroach Bonus Content

Frog (Rana tigrina) Deep Analysis:

  • Skin: Highly vascular, moist (Cutaneous respiration). Mucus glands present.
  • Heart: 3 chambers (2 atria + 1 ventricle). Sinus venosus and Conus arteriosus present.
  • Excretion: Ureotelic. Kidneys are mesonephric.
  • Reproduction: External fertilisation in water. Larva = Tadpole (Ammonotelic).
  • Special: Males have vocal sacs and copulatory pads on the first digit of forelimbs.

Cockroach (Periplaneta americana) Highlights:

  • Exoskeleton: Chitinous sclerites (Dorsal-Tergites, Ventral-Sternites).
  • Vision: Mosaic vision (4000 ommatidia) — High sensitivity but low resolution.
  • Circulation: 13-chambered tubular heart. Open system.
  • Respiration: Tracheal system. 10 pairs of spiracles.

🎯 Enzyme Master Map
  • Saliva: Salivary Amylase (Ptyalin) — Starch to Maltose (pH 6.8).
  • Stomach: Pepsin (Proteins → Peptones), Rennin (Infants, Milk protein).
  • Pancreas: Trypsinogen, Chymotrypsinogen, Procarboxypeptidases, Amylases, Lipases, Nucleases.
  • Intestine: Dipeptidases, Maltase, Lactase, Sucrase, Nucleosidases.

Absorption Logic: Glucose/AA = Active or Facilitated. Fructose = Facilitated. Fats/Glycerol = Micelles → Chylomicrons → Lacteals (Lymph).

🎯 O2-Dissociation Curve Analysis

A Right Shift happens when Oxygen affinity decreases (more unloading). Triggers: ↑ pCO2, ↑ [H+]/↓ pH, ↑ Temperature, ↑ 2,3-BPG.

Transport of CO2: 70% as Bicarbonate ions, 20-25% as Carbamino-hemoglobin, 7% as dissolved state in Plasma.

Excretory Products & Elimination

The Nephron Engine
  • PCT: 70-80% electrolytes & water reabsorbed. Main site of pH balance.
  • Loop of Henle: Descending = permeable to Water (concentrates filtrate). Ascending = permeable to Electrolytes (dilutes filtrate).
  • DCT: Conditional reabsorption of Na+ & Water under ADH/Aldosterone.
🎯 RAAS Mechanism (The Blood Pressure Rescue)

↓ BP → JGA cells release Renin → Angiotensinogen to Angiotensin I → II. Angiotensin II is a powerful vasoconstrictor and triggers Aldosterone (Na+ reabsorption).

Locomotion & Movement

Sliding Filament Theory: During contraction, H-zone narrows/disappears, I-band shortens, A-band remains constant. Ca++ binds to Troponin to expose active sites on Actin.

Joint Master List (NEET Must-Know)

Ball & Socket: Shoulder/Hip · Hinge: Knee/Elbow · Pivot: Atlas/Axis · Gliding: Carpals · Saddle: Carpal & Metacarpal of Thumb

Neural Control & Coordination

Impulse Conduction: Resting state = -70mV (K+ permeable, Na+ tight). Depolarization = Na+ influx (channels open). Repolarization = K+ efflux.

🎯 Parasympathetic vs Sympathetic

Sympathetic (Fight/Flight) = ↑ Heart rate, Dilate pupil, Inhibits digestion. Parasympathetic (Rest/Digest) = ↓ Heart rate, Constrict pupil, Stimulates digestion.

Reproduction & Health
Anatomy Menstrual Cycle Health & ART

Anatomy & Gametogenesis Critical Unit

Male: Testis (Scrotum, 2-2.5°C lower) → Seminiferous Tubules (Sperm production) → Rete Testis → Vasa efferentia → Epididymis → Vas deferens.

Female: Ovary → Fallopian Tube (Infundibulum, Ampulla, Isthmus) → Uterus → Cervix.

Spermatogenesis vs Oogenesis
  • Spermatogenesis: Starts at puberty. Continuous. 1 spermatogonium → 4 sperms.
  • Oogenesis: Starts at embryonic stage. Discontinuous (stops at Prophase I until puberty, then Metaphase II until fertilisation). 1 oogonium → 1 ovum + polar bodies.

The Menstrual Cycle Engine SVG Analysis

🎯 Day-by-Day Logic

Proliferative Phase (Day 5-13): Estrogen ↑. Regeneration of Endometrium. Ovulation (Day 14): Peak of LH, FSH, and Estrogen. Luteal Phase (Day 15-28): Progesterone ↑ (secreted by Corpus Luteum).
