🔄 Alternation of Generations — Key Visual

🎯 NEET Master Principle — Life Cycles

Gametophyte dominant → Algae & Bryophytes. Sporophyte dominant → Pteridophytes, Gymnosperms, Angiosperms. In Algae, both phases can be free-living (isomorphic) or one can dominate (heteromorphic). In Bryophytes, sporophyte is partially/fully dependent on gametophyte. In Pteridophytes, gametophyte (prothallus) is free-living but very small. Both phases are free-living in Pteridophytes — this is the NEET trap!

⚡ 5-Second Decision Engine — Plant Kingdom

See "dominant generation" → If gametophyte: pick Algae/Bryophyta. If sporophyte: pick Pteridophyta/Gymno/Angio.
See "needs water for fertilisation" → eliminate Gymnosperms & Angiosperms → ONLY Algae/Bryophyta/Pteridophyta.
See "naked seeds" → Gymnosperm ONLY. See "double fertilisation" → Angiosperm ONLY.
See "vascular tissue absent" → eliminate Pteridophyta, Gymno, Angio → only Algae or Bryophyta remain.

Trap: Examiner may show "Chlamydomonas = diploid dominant" — FALSE. | Fucus: Diplontic cycle — dominant sporophyte, gametes are only haploid. | Ectocarpus: Haplo-diplontic — both free-living. NEET asks "which algae has isomorphic alternation of generations?" → Ectocarpus.

⚠ Most Tested NEET Trap — Gametophyte vs Sporophyte

Moss (Funaria): Sporophyte (capsule on stalk) is dependent on gametophyte. Fern: Both free-living — this question has appeared 3+ times directly. Seed plants: Female gametophyte is the embryo sac (7 cells, 8 nuclei) — highly reduced, dependent.

Algae — All Three Classes 8+ times in NEET

Algae are chlorophyll-bearing, simple, thalloid, autotrophic, and largely aquatic organisms. They may be unicellular (Chlamydomonas) to filamentous (Spirogyra) to colonial (Volvox). They fix ~50% of global CO₂.

📋 PYQs — Algae

NEET 2022 In which of the following, is the reserve food material "Floridean starch" found?
Answer: Rhodophyceae (Red Algae)

Key: Floridean starch is characteristic of Red algae. It resembles amylopectin (branched). Green algae store true starch, Brown algae store laminarin and mannitol. This has appeared multiple times — know all three reserve foods.

NEET 2020 Which one of the following algae is used as a source of agar-agar?
Answer: Gelidium and Gracilaria (Red algae)

Agar-agar is a polysaccharide extracted from red algae Gelidium and Gracilaria. It is used in microbiological culture media, as food additive, and in ice cream production. Trap: Do not confuse with algin (Brown) or carrageen (Red).

NEET 2019 Identify the algal example where sexual reproduction occurs by conjugation:
Answer: Spirogyra

In Spirogyra, the two filaments align side by side (scalariform conjugation) or cells of the same filament conjugate (lateral conjugation). The gametes lack flagella — this is isogamous but non-motile. No flagellated cells in Spirogyra despite being green algae.

Bryophyta — Amphibians of Plant Kingdom 5–6 Q/decade

Key Definition

Bryophytes are called "amphibians of plant kingdom" because they need water for sexual reproduction (flagellated spermatozoids) but can survive on land. No vascular tissue. Gametophyte is dominant; sporophyte is partially/fully dependent on gametophyte.

NCERT Direct: Sphagnum (peat moss) provides peat that can be used as fuel, and is also of great economic importance. Marchantia reproduces vegetatively through gemma — a specialised vegetative body formed in gemma cups on the dorsal surface of thallus. The sporophyte of bryophytes is not able to live independently.

Bryophyte sporophyte dependency: In Riccia = most dependent (embedded in gametophyte, no seta). In Pellia/Marchantia = partially dependent. In liverworts generally: foot only for attachment. In Mosses: foot + seta + capsule — capsule has stomata and photosynthesises partially. Only bryophyte sporophyte with stomata = Funaria capsule.

NEET 2023 In which of the following, gemmae (vegetative reproductive structure) are found in cup-like structures called gemma cups?
Answer: Marchantia

Gemma cups are small cup-like structures seen on the dorsal surface of Marchantia thallus. Gemmae (disc-like multicellular asexual bodies) are produced in these cups and get detached to form new individuals. This is vegetative reproduction.

Pteridophyta — First Vascular Land Plants 4+ times

Key Features

Pteridophytes are the first terrestrial plants to possess vascular tissue (xylem and phloem). Sporophyte is dominant and independent. Gametophyte (prothallus) is free-living, photosynthetic, monoecious. Fertilisation still requires water. First plant group with Leaves (microphylls in Selaginella; megaphylls in ferns).

🎯 Heterospory — Critical NEET Concept

Heterospory (production of two kinds of spores — microspores and megaspores) is first seen in Pteridophytes (Selaginella, Salvinia). This is the precursor to seed habit. The megaspore germinates into female gametophyte; microspore into male gametophyte — all within the spore wall. This is the evolutionary step towards seeds.

NCERT Direct: Gametophyte of pteridophytes is called prothallus. It is small, inconspicuous, free-living, and mostly photosynthetic. Prothallus bears both antheridia (male) and archegonia (female). The prothallus requires cool, damp conditions. Marsilea is heterosporous and aquatic.

NEET 2021 Identify the correct statement about the fern gametophyte (prothallus):
Answer: It is free-living, photosynthetic and haploid

The prothallus is a free-living, autotrophic (photosynthetic) haploid structure — the gametophyte generation of ferns. It bears antheridia (producing biflagellate antherozoids) and archegonia (producing the egg). Fertilisation occurs in the presence of water. The zygote (2n) develops into the sporophyte — the familiar fern plant.

Gymnosperms & Angiosperms 5+ times each

Gymnosperms
  • Naked seeds (ovules not enclosed in ovary)
  • Pollen tube — no water needed for fertilisation
  • Microsporophylls (♂) & megasporophylls (♀) in cones (strobili)
  • Mostly evergreen; needle-like leaves — reduced transpiration
  • Cycas: only living gymnosperm with coralloid roots (house Anabaena — N₂ fixing)
  • Pinus: resin ducts; two types of branches (long + dwarf)
  • Ephedra: source of drug ephedrine
  • Sequoia: tallest living tree
Angiosperms
  • Seeds enclosed in fruits (carpel/ovary)
  • Flowers present — most diverse plant group
  • Double fertilisation — unique feature
  • Triploid endosperm (3n) — nutritive tissue
  • Monocots: one cotyledon, parallel venation, fibrous root
  • Dicots: two cotyledons, reticulate venation, tap root
  • Class Monocotyledonae & Dicotyledonae

NCERT Direct — Gymnosperm reproduction: In Pinus, the male cones (microsporangiate) bear microsporophylls each with 2 microsporangia. The microsporangia produce pollen grains (microspores). The female cone (megasporangiate) bears megasporophylls, each with 2 naked ovules. Wind pollination (anemophily). Pollen tube carries male gametes to archegonium.

Monocot vs Dicot — Quick Fire (NEET 5 direct Qs)

Monocot: 1 cotyledon, parallel venation, fibrous/adventitious root, trimerous flower (petals in 3s), closed vascular bundles, no secondary growth (except Dracaena/Yucca as exception). Dicot: 2 cotyledons, reticulate venation, tap root, tetramerous/pentamerous flower, open vascular bundles, secondary growth present.

NEET 2024 In which of the following plants are the ovules naked (not enclosed in an ovary)?
Answer: Pinus (Gymnosperm)

In Gymnosperms, the ovules are not enclosed within an ovary — they are borne directly on megasporophylls. The word "Gymnosperm" means "naked seed" (Greek: gymnos = naked, sperma = seed). In Angiosperms, ovules are enclosed in the carpel/ovary.

⚠ NEET Classic — Coconut edible part?

Coconut: Drupe. Coir = mesocarp fibres. Coconut shell = hard endocarp. White kernel = endosperm (edible solid part). Coconut water = coconut milk = liquid endosperm (nuclear type, free-nuclear stage). The "coconut" we eat (shell with husk) = fruit. Coconut water is NOT endosperm per se — it's free nuclei in liquid before cellular endosperm forms. The hard white part (kernel) = solid/cellular endosperm.

🔬 Diffusion, Osmosis & Water Potential

Water Potential (ψ)

ψ = ψs + ψp

ψs = solute potential (always negative — solutes lower water potential)

ψp = pressure potential (usually positive, helps turgor; negative in xylem under tension)

Pure water: ψ = 0. Water moves from high ψ → low ψ (high to low water potential = from dilute to concentrated).

🎯 Cohesion-Tension — NEET Explanation

Step 1: Transpiration from leaves creates water deficit → tension (negative pressure) in mesophyll cells. Step 2: This suction transmitted down through continuous water column in xylem vessels via cohesion (H-bonding between water molecules). Step 3: Adhesion to xylem walls prevents column break. Step 4: Root absorbs water to replace what's lost. Water potential gradient: root > stem > leaf > atmosphere.

🔄 Phloem Transport — Pressure Flow / Mass Flow

Munch's Pressure Flow Hypothesis (1930)

Source (leaf): Photosynthesis → sucrose → actively loaded into phloem SE → decreases ψ → water from xylem enters by osmosis → turgor pressure increases.

Sink (root/storage): Sucrose unloaded → ψ increases → water returns to xylem → turgor drops.

Result: Pressure gradient from source to sink drives mass flow of sap in phloem. Phloem transport is bidirectional (unlike xylem which is unidirectional upward). Sucrose is the main form of transport in phloem.

NEET 2022 Which of the following transport is responsible for bidirectional flow in plants?
Answer: Phloem transport

Phloem transports organic solutes (sucrose) bidirectionally — from source to sink. A leaf at the bottom can act as source for roots (downward) while a leaf at the top acts as source for growing apical meristem (upward). Xylem is strictly unidirectional (root → leaves).

NEET 2024 In C4 plants, the primary CO₂ acceptor is:
Answer: Phosphoenolpyruvate (PEP)

In C4 plants: PEP (3C) + CO₂ → OAA (4C), catalysed by PEP carboxylase in mesophyll cells. OAA is then converted to malate or aspartate and transported to bundle sheath cells. In contrast, C3 plants use RuBP as CO₂ acceptor. The question is a classic NEET pattern.

NEET 2022 Which of the following is NOT produced in the light reactions of photosynthesis?
Answer: Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆)

Light reactions produce: ATP, NADPH, O₂. Glucose is produced in the dark reactions (Calvin cycle) using ATP and NADPH. The light reaction products (ATP + NADPH) are called assimilatory powers. G3P (glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate) is the first carbohydrate, not glucose directly.

🎯 Photoperiodism Key Concept (NEET 2022, 2020)

Critical factor = dark period (night period), NOT day length! An SDP flowers when the night > critical length. Interrupting the night period with even brief light exposure prevents flowering in SDP but promotes it in LDP. Vernalisation

Vernalisation = Low temperature treatment given to seeds/plants to promote early flowering. Wheat, barley (winter varieties) require cold exposure. The perception of cold is in shoot apex. Hyoscyamus niger (henbane) is a quantitative LDP that also requires vernalisation — this is a classic NEET example. Devernalisation = exposure to high temperature cancels the effect.

NEET 2023 Which plant growth regulator stimulates the synthesis of α-amylase in germinating barley seeds?
Answer: Gibberellins (GA₃)

In germinating barley (and other cereals), gibberellins secreted by the embryo stimulate the aleurone layer cells to synthesise and secrete α-amylase enzyme. α-amylase hydrolyses starch in the endosperm → maltose → glucose → energy for germination. This is a classic Paleg (1960) experiment documented in NCERT.

NEET 2024 Apical dominance is caused by:
Answer: High Auxin (IAA) concentration from shoot apex

Auxin produced by the apical meristem (shoot tip) diffuses downward. In lateral buds, this high auxin concentration inhibits their growth — apical dominance. Removal of shoot tip (decapitation) → axillary buds grow out (bushier plant). Cytokinins counter apical dominance. This principle is used in gardening (pinching off tips).

🎯 Double Fertilisation — Exact Terms (NEET Must Know)

NEET 2024 After double fertilisation, the primary endosperm nucleus is:
Answer: Triploid (3n)

PEN = one male gamete (n) + two polar nuclei (n + n) = 3n. It then undergoes free nuclear divisions to form endosperm. Modern textbooks: central cell of embryo sac contains the 2 polar nuclei (not yet secondary nucleus — they fuse during fertilisation). The endosperm ploidy = 3n = triploid. Coconut water = liquid endosperm (free nuclear stage).

The synergids have a special structure called the filiform apparatus at their micropylar end. This apparatus, made of cell wall ingrowths, is believed to help direct the pollen tube into the embryo sac and may secrete chemotropic substances. The synergid disintegrates as pollen tube enters.

❌ Cytokinin — delays senescence; promotes opening (indirect)

  • "C4 first product = PGA" → NO. OAA is first C4 product (in mesophyll)
  • "Sugarcane = C3" → NO. Sugarcane is C4 (most tested C4 plant)
  • "Stomata open at night" → FALSE for C3/C4. TRUE for CAM plants
  • "Glycolysis in mitochondria" → IN CYTOPLASM; no mitochondria needed
  • "Krebs cycle on inner membrane" → NO. Krebs = matrix; ETC = inner membrane
  • "CO₂ comes from CO₂ in photosynthesis" → NO. O₂ comes from H₂O
  • "ABA opens stomata" → OPPOSITE. ABA CLOSES stomata
  • "Endosperm = 2n" → WRONG. Endosperm = 3n (triploid)
  • "Fruit = pericarp + seed" → YES but examiners test seed vs fruit frequently
  • "Tapetum undergoes meiosis" → NO. MMC (PMC) does
  • "Pollination = fertilisation" → NO. Pollination = pollen transfer; fertilisation = gamete fusion
  • "Apomixis = parthenogenesis in plants" → SIMILAR but apomixis = broader term
  • "Bryophytes have dominant sporophyte" → WRONG. Gametophyte dominates in bryophytes
  • "Photoperiodism controlled by light duration" → DARK period is what actually matters
  • "Auxin applied to cut stem = promotes new apex" → promotes root, not shoot
  • "All seeds are anatropous" → Most angiosperms are, but not universal
  • "ψ of pure water = negative" → WRONG. ψ of pure water = 0
  • "Both fertilisations in angiosperms = double fertilisation" → Correct definition; syngamy + triple fusion
